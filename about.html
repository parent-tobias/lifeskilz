ABOUT ED MACK

What qualifies a person to be a “life coach”? Is it about where they studied? How many certifications they have and organizations they belong to? Just how does a person prepare or study to assist others in being the best they can be in a very cold and demanding world? I believe first and foremost the gifts required to coach, mentor, and counsel others are given by God. The day we were born and our life experiences coupled with education, allow for the development of those skilz. Some people know what they want to be when they grow up early on, but the majority of us figure this out as we live our lives. In my life experience I am currently or have been the following:

Son, Brother, Husband, Father, Grandfather, Friend, Student, Employee, Supervisor, Team Leader, Airman, Noncommissioned Officer In Charge, Division Head, Director, Cell Group Leader, Cell Group Director, Teacher, Mentor, Minister, Life Coach, Executive Pastor and most importantly a child of God and Teacher of Life Skilz.

Click here to learn more about me!

I believe the Bible is the ultimate manual for instruction on how to live in this world. With correct understanding devoid of traditional “religious” traps and bondages its principles are accurate and true and applying its principles will guarantee a successful and fulfilling life.

I have been given unique gifts, talents, and insights in order to observe and get a great understanding of human nature in my time on this earth. Not the type of information taught in schools of higher learning with their theories and hypotheses, but real life experiences of myself and others I have been privileged to be around. I take these life experiences along with a fresh understanding of the Bible absent of “religion” and use it to equip others to experience a more abundant life and navigate them away from many of the pitfalls and traps life can offer.

Although my background comes from a Judeo-Christian, nondenominational, nontraditional religious perspectives, I believe I have much to offer those who do not believe as I do and from a nonjudgmental place. My job is to live and share what I know and it is your job to decide as to whether what I have to offer will work for you. We are all given free will and free choice.
